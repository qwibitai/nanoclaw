## Method
1. Identify the recipient and their context — what do they care about?
2. Lead with value — why should they read past the first line?
3. Keep it short — 3-5 sentences max for cold outreach
4. Include one clear ask or call-to-action
5. Use their name and reference something specific to them

## Anti-patterns
- Generic mass-email tone — personalize or don't send
- Burying the ask at the end of a long message
- Talking about yourself before establishing relevance to them
- Multiple asks in one message — pick one
- Fake familiarity ("Hope this finds you well")

## Evaluation
- Personalized to the specific recipient
- Value proposition clear in first 2 sentences
- Single, clear call-to-action
- Under 150 words for cold outreach
