# Indian Geography Fundamentals

## Objective

Build a reliable spatial and physical geography base for UPSC prelims — the layer of geographic facts that recur across geography, environment, agriculture, and current affairs questions.

## Core Ideas

- Geography questions in UPSC test location, explanation, and inter-relationships — not just naming.
- Physical geography provides the underlying logic for climate, rivers, soil, and agriculture patterns.
- The map is your friend: most geography errors in UPSC come from vague spatial recall.

## High-Yield Anchors

1. Physiographic Divisions of India
- Five major zones: Himalayan mountains, Northern plains, Peninsular plateau, Coastal plains, Island groups.
- Himalayas: three parallel ranges (Greater/Himadri, Lesser/Himachal, Outer/Shiwalik), their characteristics, and why they block northern cold winds.
- Northern plains: formed by Indus, Ganga, and Brahmaputra systems; Khadar (younger alluvium) vs Bhangar (older alluvium).

2. Drainage Systems
- The Himalayan river systems: Indus, Ganga, and Brahmaputra — antecedent rivers, perennial, snow-fed.
- The Peninsular river systems: rain-fed, non-perennial, flow east (Bay of Bengal) or west (Arabian Sea).
- East-flowing east: Mahanadi, Godavari, Krishna, Cauvery — form deltas. West-flowing: Narmada, Tapi — rift valleys, no deltas.

3. Climate Patterns
- India's climate is monsoon-dominated: southwest monsoon (June–September) and northeast monsoon (October–December).
- Western Ghats: windward vs leeward — rainfall disparity is a recurring question.
- Rain shadow areas: Deccan Plateau, Rajasthan — understand the mechanism, not just the location.

4. Soils of India
- Alluvial soil: most widespread, highly fertile, Northern Plains and river deltas.
- Black cotton soil (Regur): self-ploughing, retains moisture well, Deccan, Maharashtra — cotton and soybean.
- Laterite soil: leached of nutrients, tropical highlands — poor for crops except plantation.
- Red soil: low moisture retention, less fertile, common in Telangana, Tamil Nadu interiors.

5. Natural Vegetation Zones
- Tropical rainforests: Western Ghats, Andaman — high rainfall, dense evergreen canopy.
- Deciduous forests: most of peninsular India — seasonal shedding, teak, sal.
- Thorn and scrub: Rajasthan, semi-arid zones — adapted to low rainfall.
- Mangroves: Sundarbans, Mahanadi delta — saline tolerance, root adaptations, biodiversity hotspots.

## Common UPSC Confusions

- Mixing antecedent and consequent rivers: Himalayan rivers predate the mountains; Peninsular rivers follow the terrain.
- Treating all western-flowing rivers as forming deltas — Narmada and Tapi form estuaries, not deltas.
- Confusing red soil distribution with laterite soil distribution.
