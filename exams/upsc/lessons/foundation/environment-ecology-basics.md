# Environment and Ecology Basics

## Objective

Build a minimal but accurate ecology vocabulary for UPSC prelims — ecosystem concepts, biodiversity frameworks, pollution types, and climate change basics that recur across Paper I and Paper II.

## Core Ideas

- Ecology questions in UPSC test relationships, not isolated definitions.
- Keep three frameworks active: food chains/pyramids, biodiversity and its loss, and climate-pollution policy.
- A working knowledge of India-specific ecosystems (Western Ghats, Sundarban, Himalayan) is expected.

## High-Yield Anchors

1. Ecosystem Basics
- Ecosystem = biotic community + abiotic environment in interaction.
- Energy flow: always one-way (sun → producers → consumers → decomposers). Matter cycles; energy does not.
- Food chain vs food web: webs are more realistic; removal of one species has cascading effects.
- Ten-percent law (Lindemann): only ~10% of energy passes from one trophic level to the next.

2. Ecological Pyramids
- Pyramid of numbers: can be inverted (e.g., one oak tree → many insects → fewer birds).
- Pyramid of biomass: can be inverted in aquatic systems (small phytoplankton support large zooplankton).
- Pyramid of energy: always upright — energy is lost at every trophic level as heat.

3. Biodiversity
- Three levels: genetic diversity, species diversity, ecosystem diversity.
- India as a megadiverse country: one of 17, despite covering only 2.4% of earth's surface.
- Hotspots in India: Western Ghats, Eastern Himalayas, Indo-Burma region — defined by both endemism and threat.
- IUCN categories: Extinct, Extinct in Wild, Critically Endangered, Endangered, Vulnerable, Near Threatened, Least Concern.

4. Conservation Approaches
- In-situ: within natural habitat — national parks, wildlife sanctuaries, biosphere reserves, Ramsar sites.
- Ex-situ: outside natural habitat — zoos, seed banks, botanical gardens, gene banks.
- Project Tiger, Project Elephant, Project Dolphin — conservation milestones and coverage.

5. Climate Change Basics
- Greenhouse gases: CO₂, CH₄, N₂O, water vapour, ozone — each with different global warming potentials.
- The Paris Agreement (2015): keep warming below 2°C, pursue 1.5°C; nationally determined contributions (NDCs).
- India's climate commitments: Net Zero by 2070, 500 GW renewable energy by 2030, PANCHAMRIT targets.

6. Pollution Types
- Air: criteria pollutants (PM2.5, PM10, SO₂, NOₓ, CO, Ozone) and their health effects.
- Water: point source vs non-point source; biochemical oxygen demand (BOD) as a water quality measure.
- Soil: heavy metals, persistent organic pollutants, plastic micro-fragments — persistence and bioaccumulation.

## Common UPSC Confusions

- Treating in-situ and ex-situ conservation as interchangeable — they serve different species and threats.
- Mixing the purpose of biosphere reserves (includes core, buffer, transition zone) with wildlife sanctuaries.
- Assuming the pyramid of biomass is always upright — aquatic ecosystems provide the key exception.
