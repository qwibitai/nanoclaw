# Modern India History Foundations

## Objective

Build a working map of India from 1757 to 1947 so UPSC prelims questions on the nationalist movement, colonial policies, and constitutional developments have a reliable mental framework.

## Core Ideas

- Colonial history is tested through causes, consequences, and comparisons — not just dates.
- UPSC frequently asks which policy or act created which outcome, so map causes to effects.
- The nationalist movement is best understood through phases: early moderates, Bal-Pal-Lal extremism, Gandhian mass mobilisation, and final transfer of power.

## High-Yield Anchors

1. Establishment and Expansion of British Rule
- Battle of Plassey (1757) and Battle of Buxar (1764) as turning points.
- Doctrine of Lapse and how it expanded territorial control.
- The Subsidiary Alliance system and why it was preferred over direct annexation initially.

2. Economic Impact of British Rule
- De-industrialisation of Indian handicrafts, especially textiles.
- Drain of wealth theory: Dadabhai Naoroji's framing.
- Land revenue systems: Zamindari, Ryotwari, Mahalwari — their regions and consequences for peasants.

3. Social and Religious Reform Movements
- Raja Ram Mohan Roy and the Bengal Renaissance.
- Brahmo Samaj, Arya Samaj, and Ramakrishna Mission — reform agendas and social impact.
- These are tested through their role in creating a reform-minded middle class.

4. The Rise of Indian Nationalism
- Early Congress (1885): moderate demands, petition politics.
- Partition of Bengal (1905) as a catalyst for extremism and Swadeshi.
- Bal Gangadhar Tilak's "Swaraj is my birthright" and the shift toward mass participation.

5. Gandhi and Mass Movements
- Non-Cooperation Movement (1920–1922): causes, strategy, withdrawal.
- Civil Disobedience Movement (1930–1934): Dandi March, significance of salt.
- Quit India Movement (1942): August Kranti and its difference from earlier movements.

6. Constitutional Milestones
- Government of India Acts: 1919 (dyarchy) and 1935 (provincial autonomy, federation that never happened).
- Cabinet Mission Plan and why it failed.
- Mountbatten Plan and partition logic.

## Common UPSC Confusions

- Mixing provisions of GoI Acts 1919 and 1935.
- Treating all Gandhi-era movements as identical in strategy and scope.
- Forgetting the difference between Home Rule League (Tilak, Besant) and the formal Congress sessions.
