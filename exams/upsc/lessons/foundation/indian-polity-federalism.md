# Indian Polity Federalism Basics

## Objective

Build a practical first-pass understanding of Indian federalism for UPSC prelims so constitutional structure, Centre-State relations, and recurring traps become easier to recall.

## Core Ideas

- India is constitutionally described as a Union of States, which means it has federal features without being a classic federation in the U.S. sense.
- UPSC often tests the balance between distribution of powers and the stronger position granted to the Union in exceptional or structural situations.
- Read federalism through institutions: Seventh Schedule, Finance Commission, Inter-State Council, Governor, Parliament, and the Supreme Court.

## High-Yield Anchors

1. Constitutional Structure
- Know why India is called a federal system with unitary bias.
- Focus on written Constitution, division of powers, supremacy of Constitution, and independent judiciary.

2. Seventh Schedule
- Union List, State List, and Concurrent List are the core map for legislative competence.
- UPSC repeatedly tests whether a topic belongs to one list or another, especially where confusion with Concurrent List exists.

3. Residuary Powers and Emergencies
- Residuary powers lie with the Union, unlike some classic federations.
- Emergency provisions can temporarily strengthen the Centre further and are a common comparison point in MCQs.

4. Fiscal Federalism
- Understand the role of the Finance Commission, GST Council, and tax devolution.
- Questions may test cooperative versus competitive federalism through current institutional examples.

5. Governor and Centre-State Friction
- The Governor is a frequent UPSC theme because it sits at the intersection of constitutional design and political practice.
- Revise discretionary powers carefully and do not overstate them.

## Common UPSC Confusions

- Assuming India is either purely federal or purely unitary.
- Mixing residuary powers with Concurrent List subjects.
- Treating the Governor as only a ceremonial post in all contexts.

## Quick Recall Drill

- Why is India often called federal with unitary bias?
- Where do residuary powers lie in the Indian Constitution?
- Which institution recommends tax devolution between the Centre and States?
