# Indian Economy Basics

## Objective

Build foundational economic literacy for UPSC prelims — not academic economics, but the practical vocabulary and concepts that appear in Prelims Paper I and the Economy section.

## Core Ideas

- UPSC economy questions test applied understanding: how a policy works, not just its name.
- Focus on five anchors: planning, agriculture, industry, money and banking, external sector.
- Current affairs in economy make sense only with a solid conceptual base.

## High-Yield Anchors

1. Types of Economies and Planning
- Mixed economy: India's model — private enterprise alongside state planning.
- Five-Year Plans: abolished in 2014, replaced by NITI Aayog and aspirational documents.
- Key distinction: NITI Aayog vs Planning Commission in function, authority, and fund-flow.

2. National Income Concepts
- GDP, GNP, NDP, NNP: understand the differences and when to use each.
- GDP at market prices vs factor cost; nominal vs real GDP.
- Base year revisions in India's national accounts and why they change the figures.

3. Agriculture
- Green Revolution: wheat-rice belt, Punjab-Haryana, HYV seeds, Borlaug's contribution.
- Agriculture's share in GDP (~16%) vs employment (~45%): structural dualism.
- Minimum Support Price (MSP): what it covers, who fixes it, and its fiscal implications.

4. Money, Banking and RBI
- Functions of RBI: monetary authority, banker to government, regulator of banks.
- Monetary policy instruments: repo rate, reverse repo, CRR, SLR.
- Priority sector lending: which sectors qualify and why.

5. Government Budget
- Revenue vs capital expenditure: recurring costs vs asset creation.
- Fiscal deficit = total expenditure − total receipts (excluding borrowings).
- Primary deficit = fiscal deficit − interest payments: the "new borrowing" measure.

6. External Sector
- Current account: trade in goods, services, income, and transfers.
- Capital account: FDI, FII/FPI, ECB — definitions and policy significance.
- Foreign exchange reserves: why they matter for import cover and currency stability.

## Common UPSC Confusions

- Mixing GNP and GDP when adjusting for net factor income from abroad.
- Treating CRR and SLR as equivalent; they serve different purposes and go to different places.
- Assuming fiscal deficit and revenue deficit are the same measure.
